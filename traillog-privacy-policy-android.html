Traillog Privacy Policy

Android edition · Last updated: 2026-04-29

Traillog is a location timeline and fitness tracking app. This policy explains what data the Android app uses, how it is stored, and how you control it. The app is published by Weganar.

Data We Process

Depending on the features you use, Traillog may process:

  • Precise and approximate location data (to record your trips and build your timeline).
  • Motion and activity recognition data (to detect walking, running, cycling, and driving).
  • Health and fitness data from Health Connect (heart rate, steps, distance, calories, exercise sessions, VO2 max, sleep, blood oxygen, resting heart rate, HRV, weight, height, body fat), only when the specific data type is granted.
  • Bluetooth data from paired fitness sensors (heart rate, cycling speed and cadence, cycling power, running footpods), only while you are using a paired device.
  • Photo metadata (EXIF timestamp and GPS coordinates) and references to selected photos, to match photos in your gallery to recorded trips. Photos themselves are not uploaded.
  • Optional notes, tags, and titles you add to segments.
  • Sharing metadata for people you invite to see your trips.
  • Anonymous device and crash diagnostics through Firebase Crashlytics and Analytics.

Where Data Is Stored

All recorded data is stored locally on your device in an encrypted database. If cloud sync is enabled, data is synced to Firebase Firestore using your Firebase Authentication account (anonymous, Google, or Apple). Sync is scoped so only you can read your data, and only people you explicitly share a trip with can read the subset you share.

Traillog opts out of Android Auto Backup for its core database and credentials, so your location history is not transferred to Google's backup system or to any new device without an explicit sign-in.

How Data Is Used

Data is used to:

  • Build and display your timeline (stays, trips, activities).
  • Provide statistics, summaries, trends, and map views.
  • Support optional trip sharing and the connected-people family map.
  • Support optional export and Wear OS companion features.
  • Monitor app stability through anonymous crash reports.

Background Location

Traillog collects location data to record your trips even when the app is closed or your phone is locked. Recording only runs while a trip is active, and a persistent Android notification shows you whenever location is being accessed. You can pause or stop tracking at any time from the notification without unlocking the phone. When no trip is running, Traillog does not access location in the background.

Sharing

Sharing is optional. You control when and with whom trips are shared. You can revoke access by removing connected people or deleting a share. Traillog does not sell or share your data with advertising networks, data brokers, or any third party other than the infrastructure providers named in this policy.

Permissions

Traillog requests only the permissions needed for the features you enable:

  • Location (foreground and background)
  • Activity Recognition
  • Bluetooth scan and connect (for fitness sensors)
  • Photos (Android 13 and later: Read media images)
  • Notifications (Android 13 and later)
  • Health Connect read and write access, granted individually per data type

You can revoke any permission at any time in Android Settings, or the specific data types in the Health Connect app.

Data Retention and Control

You can:

  • Stop tracking at any time.
  • Configure how long location points are retained on device.
  • Export your data (where available).
  • Remove shared access by deleting shares.
  • Delete your cloud account and its data from Settings → Account → Delete account. This removes your Firestore data and signs you out of all devices.

Diagnostics and Crash Reporting

Crash reports and anonymous usage metrics are sent to Firebase Crashlytics and Firebase Analytics. Traillog redacts user, trip, share, and account identifiers and email addresses from crash logs before upload. Diagnostic collection is opt-out and can be disabled in Settings.

Third-Party Services

Traillog uses the following services as part of app functionality:

  • Google Firebase Authentication, Firestore, Crashlytics, and Analytics (for accounts, cloud sync, and diagnostics).
  • Google Play Services Location and Activity Recognition (for GPS and motion).
  • Google Maps SDK for Android (for map views).
  • Health Connect by Google (for fitness data exchange).
  • Google Weather API (for weather enrichment of recorded segments).
  • Google Play Services Wearable Data Layer (for Wear OS companion support).

Children's Privacy

Traillog is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

Changes to This Policy
